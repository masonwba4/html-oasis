Standing on the Shoulder of Giants (2000)

Release Date: February 28, 2000

"Standing on the Shoulder of Giants" marked a period of change for Oasis, being their first album without founding members Bonehead and Guigsy. The album saw the band experimenting with more psychedelic and electronic influences, signaling a departure from their earlier Britpop sound. It features the hit single "Go Let It Out" and the Noel-sung "Where Did It All Go Wrong?".

While met with a more varied critical response than their earlier work, it demonstrated Oasis's willingness to evolve their sound and explored new lyrical themes. It remains an important album in their discography for its transitional nature.
