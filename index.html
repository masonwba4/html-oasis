The Unmistakable Sound of Oasis

Oasis was an English rock band formed in Manchester in 1991. Initially known as the Rain, the group rose to prominence in the mid-1990s as a leading act of the Britpop movement. With their anthemic rock songs, swaggering attitude, and well-publicized sibling rivalry between primary songwriters Noel Gallagher and lead singer Liam Gallagher, Oasis became one of the most successful and influential bands of their era.

Their sound was characterized by catchy melodies, powerful guitar riffs, and Liam's distinctive vocal delivery. They achieved massive commercial success, selling over 75 million records worldwide and scoring numerous number-one singles and albums.

Key Info

  • Active: 1991–2009
  • Genre: Britpop, Alternative Rock, Rock
  • Origin: Manchester, England
  • Notable Members: Liam Gallagher, Noel Gallagher, Paul "Bonehead" Arthurs, Paul "Guigsy" McGuigan, Alan White

Best Albums

While opinions vary, these are generally considered some of Oasis's most iconic and influential albums:

  • Definitely Maybe (1994): Their debut, raw and energetic, featuring classics like "Supersonic" and "Live Forever."
  • (What's the Story) Morning Glory? (1995): Their most commercially successful album, home to "Wonderwall," "Don't Look Back in Anger," and "Champagne Supernova."
  • Be Here Now (1997): Despite mixed critical reviews at the time, it became the fastest-selling album in UK chart history and features tracks like "D'You Know What I Mean?"

The Brothers Gallagher & Others

Liam Gallagher (Vocals)

Born William John Paul Gallagher, Liam is the outspoken and charismatic frontman of Oasis. Known for his distinctive sneering vocal style and confident stage presence, he was a key figure in the band's public image. After Oasis, Liam went on to form Beady Eye and later embarked on a successful solo career.

Noel Gallagher (Lead Guitar, Songwriter, Vocals)

Born Noel Thomas David Gallagher, Noel was the principal songwriter, lead guitarist, and occasional lead vocalist for Oasis. He was responsible for penning most of the band's biggest hits and was often seen as the musical mastermind behind their success. Following Oasis's split, Noel formed Noel Gallagher's High Flying Birds, achieving continued critical and commercial acclaim.

Other Key Members:

  • Paul "Bonehead" Arthurs (Rhythm Guitar, 1991–1999): A founding member and integral part of the early Oasis sound and camaraderie.
  • Paul "Guigsy" McGuigan (Bass Guitar, 1991–1999): Another founding member, providing the solid bass lines that underpinned their early work.
  • Alan White (Drums, 1995–2004): Joined the band for their most commercially successful period, contributing to albums like "(What's the Story) Morning Glory?" and "Be Here Now."
