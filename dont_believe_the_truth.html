Don't Believe the Truth (2005)

Release Date: May 30, 2005

"Don't Believe the Truth" is often cited as one of Oasis's strongest later albums, receiving widespread critical acclaim. Similar to "Heathen Chemistry," it featured songwriting contributions from all band members, leading to a diverse and cohesive sound. The album spawned popular singles such as "Lyla," "The Importance of Being Idle," and "Let There Be Love."

The album showcased a more mature and refined Oasis, demonstrating their ability to produce compelling rock music well into their career. It was a commercial success, further solidifying their place as one of Britain's most enduring bands.
